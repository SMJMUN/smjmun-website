# SMJMUN Website — Full Content Review
*Reviewed: every page and component in mun-main (home, about, conferences, programs ×3, partnerships, contact, gallery, blog/journal, register, payment, footer, navigation, metadata).*

---

## 1. Overall Content Review

**The good news:** the voice of this website is already better than 90% of MUN websites. The About page ("An Institution Built Around Purpose", the Four Pillars, "The World Is Still Being Written") and the Partnerships page are excellent — mature, restrained, on-brand. The tagline system (Dare. Rise. Impact.) is applied consistently as a philosophy, not just a slogan. The program pages are well-structured and complete.

**The bad news:** the site currently fails on *credibility*, which for a premium MUN brand is fatal. Three systemic problems:

**A. The brand has no single identity.**
- "SMJMUN" vs "SMJ MUN" — both used interchangeably across every page. Pick ONE (recommend **SMJMUN**, matching the logo/domain) and enforce it everywhere.
- The full name appears as both "Shri Seth Mangilalji Sahu **International** Model United Nations" (site metadata) and "Shri Seth Mangilalji Sahu Model United Nations" (About page). Pick one.
- Founder is spelled "**Aarush Sahu**" throughout. Confirm official spelling (Aarush vs Aarushh).
- Mixed UK/US English: "organisations/programmes" (Partnerships) vs "organizations/programs" (elsewhere). Pick one — recommend UK English with "programs" as the exception, or full US English.

**B. The numbers contradict each other on every page.** A parent or principal who visits two pages will notice:

| Claim | Home | Conferences | Contact | School Program | College Program |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Delegates | 11,000+ | 5,000+ | 5,000+ | 11,000+ | 11,000+ ("College Delegates") |
| Partner institutions | 100+ | 100+ schools | 100+ | 150+ schools | 50+ |
| Conferences | 70+ nat'l, 10+ int'l | 20+ | 20+ | — | — |
| Countries | 20+ (Global section), 30+ (Why Attend), 15+ (Dubai card) | 30+ | 15+ | — | — |
| Years | "Since 2023" | — | — | "5+ Years of Impact" | "10+ Years" |

"Since 2023" and "10+ Years" cannot both be true. Gallery and Journal say "Est. 2024". This must be resolved with one canonical fact sheet before launch.

Also: "Best Delegate Awards — 55+" and "Committees Chaired — 50+" on the homepage Impact section are *personal founder achievements*, not institutional metrics. Mixing them into "Our Impact" confuses the story. Move them to the Founder section, or cut.

**C. Placeholder and invented content is still live in the code.**
- **Media/Press section** is entirely fictional — three unattributed articles ("How SMJ MUN Is Redefining Diplomatic Education...", "11,000 Delegates and Counting..."). No outlet names. If published as-is, this is fabricated press coverage. Replace with real coverage or remove the section.
- **Testimonials** appear invented, and one person appears twice with two different jobs: "Dr. Meera Sharma, Principal, Delhi Public School" (home) and "Dr. Meera Sharma, Delhi Public School, Indore" (school program). Every testimonial needs a real name with written permission.
- **Trusted By** logos: DPS, Heritage, Amity, Oakridge, Ryan, Sanskriti, Modern, Shiv Nadar. If any of these are not actual partners with permission, remove — this is the single biggest legal/credibility exposure on the site.
- **Conferences section** lists SMJ MUN Delhi, Dubai International, Leadership Summit (Asia-Pacific), Mumbai National. Confirm which are real.
- **Contact page office address** literally reads: *"Update with your full office address, City, State, PIN."*
- **Footer social links** point to generic facebook.com and youtube.com (only Instagram is real).
- **Footer typo**: "collage " instead of "College", plus lowercase "delegate"/"school" labels, and a link to `/resources` which doesn't exist. College program also links to a `partnership-program` slug that isn't registered (broken link).
- **World map** shows New York, London, Dubai, New Delhi, Singapore. Confirm this reflects reality.

**Missing legal pages:** the site takes live payments (HDFC gateway) but has **no Terms & Conditions, Privacy Policy, or Refund/Cancellation Policy**. Payment gateways require these; parents expect them. This is a launch blocker.

**Verdict:** the writing needs polish in places, but the priority is truth and consistency. Premium is not created by adjectives — it's created by a site where every number, name, and claim holds up.

## 2. Section-by-Section Feedback

### Home
- **Hero — "DARE. RISE. IMPACT."**: right choice as the opening statement. But it has no supporting line and the CTAs are weak ("About Us" / "Explore Programs"). A first-time parent or principal gets zero context. Add one line of positioning and make the primary CTA conversion-focused (upcoming conference or partnership).
- **About strip**: "About SMJMUN / We are SMJMUN" — the heading repeats the label. Weak. Rewrite (see §3).
- **Impact stats**: fix numbers (see above); remove personal stats from institutional impact.
- **Founder section**: "A visionary leader" — never call yourself visionary; let the record say it. The quote "A Journey Of Thousand Miles" is grammatically off ("A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step" — Lao Tzu) and sits unexplained. The claim "India's largest Model United Nations platform" is bold; only keep it if defensible.
- **Leadership Journey**: strong section. "Master the frameworks used by the world's..." — check the truncated string renders fully.
- **For Institutions**: one of the best-written sections on the site. Keep.
- **Media/In The Press**: remove or replace with real coverage (see §1C).
- **Testimonials**: replace with real, permissioned quotes. Fewer real ones beat many invented ones.
- **Journal cinematic section**: "Every story deserves to be shared" is generic greeting-card copy — the weakest headline on the site. Rewrite (see §3).
- **Global Movement**: fine, but "Inspiring dialogue. Connecting young minds. Creating global impact." is three stock phrases. Tighten.
- **CTA section**: "Shaping Tomorrow's Leaders Starts With One Conversation." — good. Keep.
- **Footer**: fix typos, dead links, socials, and add legal links. "© 2026" should be dynamic.

### About
The strongest page. "It needs thoughtful people — delegates who can listen before they argue, research before they claim..." is excellent. Four Pillars are distinctive and ownable. Two notes:
- "Roots Before Reach" description is truncated mid-sentence in code ("...before you decide where you"). Verify it renders completely.
- The closing manifesto "responsible stewards of an unfinished world" is superb — consider echoing "an unfinished world" as a recurring brand phrase (it already appears on Partnerships; good).

### Conferences
- Hero "Where Leaders Are Forged." — strong. Keep.
- "Join one of India's most distinguished Model United Nations experiences" — "one of the most distinguished" is hedged; either claim a position or say something concrete.
- Delegate Journey (registration → resolution) is clear and useful. Keep.
- Stats block contradicts homepage. Fix.
- Why Attend is solid but "Leadership Development" card appears to be missing its description — verify.

### Programs (School / College / Training)
Comprehensive, well-organized, honest about what's provided — the best conversion pages on the site. Notes:
- All three pages share near-identical structure and phrasing ("From planning to execution, every aspect..." appears twice on the same School page as both "Complete Operations" and "End-to-End Execution" — merge these cards).
- School FAQ "How many students can participate?" — answer needs a concrete number range.
- College stats say "10+ Years" and "11,000+ College Delegates" — both wrong/inflated (11,000 is the *total* across all programs, not college alone).
- "MUN Teaches You How to Speak. SMJMUN Exists to Give You Something Worth Saying." — this is your best line anywhere on the site. It's buried inside the School program's philosophy block. Promote it to the homepage.

### Partnerships
Excellent copy throughout ("Partnerships are not sponsorships. They are shared commitments..."). Only fixes: standardize UK/US spelling with the rest of the site, and the four process steps are labeled inconsistently (three unnamed + "Create Impact") — name all four (e.g., Listen → Design → Prepare → Impact).

### Contact
Well-designed and practical (WhatsApp-first is right for India). Fixes:
- Replace the placeholder address before launch.
- "Average response 15 minutes / within 24 hours" — only promise what you can keep.
- FAQ answers make operational commitments (allocations 2–3 weeks prior, certificates 2–3 weeks after, group discount at 5+ delegates). Confirm each is policy, not aspiration.

### Gallery & Journal
- Both say "Est. 2024" — conflicts with "Since 2023."
- Journal hero "India's premier Model United Nations community" — third different superlative on the site ("premier platform", "largest platform", "premier community"). Pick one positioning phrase and reuse it verbatim; repetition builds brands.
- Newsletter CTA is fine.

### Register / Payment
Functional copy is clear. Ensure the registration confirmation and closed states use the same voice ("Registration Is Currently Closed" page is good). Add a line about refund policy at point of payment once the policy page exists.

### Metadata / SEO
- Homepage title tag is good. Meta description is solid but generic; see §3.
- Add city/region keywords if SMJMUN is Indore-rooted (testimonials suggest Indore) — "MUN Indore", "MUN Madhya Pradesh" are winnable searches; "MUN India" is not (yet).

## 4. Missing Content & Recommended New Sections

1. **Legal pages (launch blocker):** Privacy Policy, Terms & Conditions, Refund & Cancellation Policy — required for the payment gateway and for parent trust.
2. **Secretariat / Team page:** delegates and schools want to see who runs conferences. Even 6–8 people with photos and roles dramatically increases trust.
3. **Real proof section:** replace fictional press with what exists — photos with named schools, award tallies, a letter from a partner principal, a downloadable conference report. One real artifact beats ten invented headlines.
4. **Code of Conduct / Safeguarding note:** one page. For school-age participants, parents and principals actively look for this; almost no Indian MUN has it. Instant differentiator.
5. **"For Parents" strip or FAQ:** what your child gains, supervision, safety, certificates. Parents pay; nobody on the site speaks to them.
6. **Alumni outcomes:** even 3 short "where are they now" stories make Dare. Rise. Impact. concrete.
7. **Conference resource hub** (`/resources` is already linked in the footer): rules of procedure, position-paper guide, sample study guide. Cheap to build from existing SMJMUN handbooks; strong SEO.
8. **Upcoming conference details:** the site's most important conversion content — dates, venue, fees, committees — should be findable in two clicks from the hero.
